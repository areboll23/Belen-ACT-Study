Five packs to build over the summer. Color-code them by subject — yellow for English, blue for Math, green for Science. Each pack tells you exactly what cards to make.

English · Pack 1
Grammar Rules
~25 cards
  • Comma rules — Oxford comma, comma splices, after introductory phrases
  • Verb tense consistency — don't mix past and present in one sentence
  • Comparative adjectives — "worse," not "more worse" · "fewer" vs "less"
  • Apostrophes — its vs it's · possession vs plural
  • Subject-verb agreement — singular subjects take singular verbs
Front of card: the rule name. Back: the rule in plain English + 1 example sentence.
English · Pack 2
Transition Words
~20 cards
  • Contrast: however · although · nevertheless · on the other hand
  • Addition: furthermore · in addition · moreover · also
  • Time: meanwhile · subsequently · previously · ultimately
  • Cause/effect: therefore · consequently · as a result · thus
  • Emphasis: indeed · in fact · notably
Front: the word. Back: what it signals + example sentence using it correctly.
Math · Pack 3
Functions
~15 cards
  • What f(x) means and how to read it
  • Evaluating a function — plug the number in for x
  • What a function graph looks like (linear, quadratic)
  • Domain = the allowed x-values · Range = the output y-values
  • Real-world word problems that use function notation
Front: the concept. Back: definition + a hand-drawn mini graph where relevant.
Science · Pack 4
Experiment Vocabulary
~20 cards
  • Independent variable — the thing you change on purpose
  • Dependent variable — the thing you measure as a result
  • Control group — the group where nothing changes (the baseline)
  • What makes an experiment a valid test
  • How to read "which evidence supports/refutes this claim" questions
Front: the term. Back: definition in your own words + a short example from everyday life.
Math · Pack 5
Stats & Probability
~15 cards
  • Mean = sum ÷ count · Median = middle value · Mode = most frequent
  • How to find median with an even number of values
  • Probability = favorable outcomes ÷ total outcomes
  • Reading bar graphs — what the x and y axes represent
  • Range = highest − lowest · Outlier = the value that doesn't fit
Front: the concept or formula. Back: worked example solved step by step.
Flashcard review schedule
Weeks 1–2
Make Pack 1 & 2 (English). Review new cards same night you make them.
Weeks 3–4
Make Pack 3, 4, 5. Review Packs 1–2 every other session to keep them fresh.
Weeks 5–6
Full review of all 5 packs before each practice section. Separate "shaky" cards.
Week 7–8
Only the shaky cards, every session. If you hesitate on a card, it stays in the shaky pile.

Study smarter, not just longer. These habits are what actually move the score.

📓
Keep an error log
Every wrong answer gets logged: the question type, what you chose, the right answer, and the rule. By August you'll have a personalized cheat sheet of exactly what needs work — and you'll see your own patterns.
🎨
Color-code the cards
Yellow cards for English, blue for Math, green for Science. It sounds small but sorting and reviewing by color is faster, and seeing the piles grow is genuinely motivating.
🗣️
Teach it back
After making a set of cards, explain the concept out loud as if you're teaching it to someone else. If you can't explain it clearly, you don't know it yet. No notes allowed during this part.
🏆
Build in rewards
Pick something fun for completing each phase — a movie night, a specific restaurant, a beach day, whatever. Finishing Phase 1 = you earned it. Extrinsic motivation is real and valid.
📵
Phone off during sessions
Not just flipped over — off. One 45-minute focused session is worth more than two hours of distracted studying. Use the Do Not Disturb setting or leave it in another room.
🔁
Space out your reviews
Don't cram a pack of cards the night before and call it done. Review English cards every 3–4 days even during Science weeks. Spaced repetition is the whole game for memorized rules.
